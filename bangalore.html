About Bangalore

Bangalore (officially Bengaluru) is the capital of Karnataka and the third-largest city in India. Known as the Garden City and the Silicon Valley of India, it is home to countless tech giants, startups, and a thriving cosmopolitan culture. With its pleasant climate, rich history, and booming economy, Bangalore attracts millions of people from across the country and the world.


Founded in 1537 by Kempe Gowda I, the city has evolved from a small princely town to a global technology hub. It houses the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), Infosys, Wipro, HAL, and hundreds of MNCs — making it the beating heart of India's IT revolution.

Culture & Heritage

Bangalore is a vibrant melting pot of traditions and modernity. Kannada is the official language, but you'll hear Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, and English spoken just as frequently. The city celebrates festivals like Ugadi, Dasara, and Karaga with great enthusiasm.


The local art scene thrives in galleries, theaters, and music venues. Craft beer breweries, live music pubs, and weekend markets sit comfortably alongside ancient temples and heritage palaces — that's the magic of Namma Bengaluru.
